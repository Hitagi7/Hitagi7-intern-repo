# Kanban Questions

**How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?**
- Kanban manages priorities and avoids overload by utilizing a "pull system" where new tasks are only started when there is available capacity, rather than having work pushed onto team members regardless of their current load. This framework helps teams focus on the most critical items by keeping the highest-priority tasks at the top of the "Ready" column, ensuring that developers always pull the most valuable work next. To prevent overload, Kanban implements Work in Progress (WIP) limits, which strictly cap the number of tasks allowed in any given stage of the workflow at one time. By enforcing these limits, the system forces the team to address bottlenecks and finish existing work before beginning anything new, which reduces the productivity loss caused by multitasking.

**How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?**
- You can improve your personal workflow using Kanban principles by strictly limiting your own "In Progress" tasks to ensure you are completing assignments rather than just starting them. By visualizing your work on a board, you can identify "hidden" tasks that are consuming your time and more effectively communicate your current capacity to your mentor or team lead. Additionally, focusing on "Cycle Time"—the time it takes to move a single task from start to finish—allows you to spot specific stages where you frequently get stuck, enabling you to seek help or adjust your approach to maintain a steady and predictable pace of work.
