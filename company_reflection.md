**Why do you think Focus Bear was created?**
- Focus Bear was created to help people with ADHD and autism (AuDHD) build consistent habits and stay focused by blocking distractions and guiding them through structured routines. Unlike generic productivity apps, it was designed with neurodivergent users in mind, people who often know what they need to do but struggle with the executive function to actually follow through. The founder himself has ADHD and autism, which means the app was born out of lived experience rather than just market research.

**What problem is Focus Bear solving?**
- Focus Bear tackles the gap between intention and action that so many people with ADHD face. It's not just about blocking distracting websites. It goes deeper by helping users build morning and evening routines, take productive breaks, and stay locked into one task at a time. What I found particularly interesting when exploring the app is the "Focus Mode" that blocks access to everything except the tools you need for your current task. That's a very different approach from apps like Freedom or Cold Turkey, which just block sites but don't actively guide you toward what you should be doing.

**Why do you think this mission is important?**
- This mission matters because ADHD is one of the most common neurodevelopmental disorders, and many people go undiagnosed or unsupported for years. I had a classmate back in university who was incredibly smart but constantly missed deadlines and would get overwhelmed trying to start assignments. They would often tell me, "I know what I need to do, I just can't make myself do it." They eventually got diagnosed with ADHD, and it was eye-opening to realize that their struggles weren't about laziness, it was about how their brain works. Tools like Focus Bear could make a real difference for people like them, providing the external structure that their brain doesn't naturally create.

**How does Focus Bear's work align with your personal values or interests?**
- I've always believed that technology should genuinely make people's lives better, not just be another source of distraction. Focus Bear aligns with that value because it uses technology to solve the distraction problem rather than add to it. On a personal level, I know what it's like to sit down to code and then realize an hour has passed because I went down a rabbit hole on YouTube or Reddit. There have been times during my studies where I'd plan to spend an evening working on a project, only to end up barely starting because I kept switching between tabs and losing my train of thought. That cycle of guilt and frustration is something I genuinely want to help solve, and it's exciting to work on a product that directly addresses it. I also find the technical side interesting building software that interacts with the OS to manage focus at a system level is a challenging and cool engineering problem.

**Do you personally relate to any of the challenges that Focus Bear aims to solve?**
- Absolutely, one specific memory that stands out is during my third year of my university days. I had a major project deadline coming up and I sat down at 7 PM to work on it. I told myself I'd just check one notification first. That turned into scrolling through social media, then watching "just one" video, and before I knew it, it was past midnight and I had barely written a single line of code. I ended up pulling an all-nighter, and the work I produced was far below what I was capable of. It wasn't that I didn't care, I cared a lot, which made the frustration even worse. That experience is exactly the kind of problem Focus Bear is designed to prevent. Features like the distraction blocker and the guided routine system would have helped me stay on track that night instead of losing hours to mindless scrolling.
