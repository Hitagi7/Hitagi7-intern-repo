**What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?**
- Some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive include the use of cluttered interfaces that expose users to over-information or high cognitive load, non-persistent navigation menus that change unpredictably, and "pushed" notifications that lack customizable sensory settings, which can trigger anxiety or overwhelm for users with Autism or ADHD.

**What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?**
- One practical change I can make as a Web Developer Intern is to always use clear HTML structure and ARIA labels. This ensures that people who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies can easily navigate and use the website.

## Identify one design or communcation improvement that could make Focus Bear more acessible

One design or communication improvement that could make Focus Bear more accessible is to implement "Low-Pressure Planning" so that whenever a user missed a task, it would automatically roll over to the next day without red "late" indicators or guilt-inducing notifications. This would help users with ADHD or Autism who struggle with multiple overdue tasks that can lead to total app abandonment. With this, the tool would become a supportive partner instead of a source of anxiety.

## Practice writing a clear, patient, and supportive response to a hypothetical user struggling with executive functioning.

**My Practice Response:**
"It is completely understandable that you're feeling overwhelmed when looking at a huge list of tasks. Instead, your brain is just looking for a clear path forward. For now, let's try to break this down and lower the pressure doing this one at a time. Fist, we will pick a tiny 'micro-task' that will take less than two minutes to complete. This 'micro-task' could be opening a document, clearing your desk, or any other small action that helps you get started and a quick win without worrying the rest of the list right now. Just know that I am here to support you, we can divide these bigger goals into smaller ones to make it more manageable in every step of the way whenever you're ready."
