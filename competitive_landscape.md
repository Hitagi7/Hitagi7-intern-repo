## Research Evidence

I explored each of the competitor apps by visiting their websites, reading through their feature pages, and reviewing user feedback. Below are my notes and observations from researching each app.

### Freedom (https://freedom.to)
- **What I found:** Freedom's features page describes itself as "Your Complete Toolkit for Distraction-Free Living." I explored 10 distinct features listed on their site: Block Apps, Block Websites, Block The Internet, Sync Across Devices, Advance Scheduling, Recurring Schedules, Blocklists, Website Exceptions, Focus Sounds, and Locked Mode.
- **Key observations:**
  - Freedom is the only blocker that I found which involves syncing blocking sessions across all devices. Basically, starting a session on your desktop or phone automatically blocks the other device. This is a standout feature that most competitors lack.
  - **Website Exceptions** mode lets you block everything except specific sites (like allowing Google Docs but blocking YouTube), which is smarter than a simple blocklist.
  - **Focus Sounds** is also observed where ambient music, café sounds, and even exclusive Brain.fm tracks to help focus, combined with Pomodoro sessions. No other competitor app I researched bundles ambient audio into a distraction blocker.
  - **Locked Mode** prevents you from quitting a session early, similar to Cold Turkey but less extreme (no computer lock/shutdown).
  - Freedom costs **$39.99/year** (Premium) and emphasizes that it has no dopamine-triggering streaks, points, or leaderboards. Since their philosophy is to reduce digital dependency, but rather gamify focus.
  - It has over **3.5 million users** worldwide.

### Cold Turkey (https://getcoldturkey.com)
- **What I found:** Cold Turkey brands itself as "The Toughest Website Blocker on the Internet.", but it is only available on desktop.

- **Key observations:**
  - **Frozen Turkey** is the most extreme feature I found across all competitors, since it can completely *lock, log off, or shut down your computer* on a schedule. This forces you to take breaks or step away. No other app offers this level of strictness.
  - **Locking options** are extensive: you can lock a block using a Timer, Random Text (you must type a long random string to unblock), Time Range, Restart requirement, or Password. This makes it nearly impossible to cheat.
  - **Block granularity** is impressive: you can block specific YouTube channels (for example: `youtube.com/-channel_name>`), specific subreddits (for example: `reddit.com/r/funny`), and even use **keyword wildcards** (for example: `google.com/*q=*unicorn*` to block Google searches for "unicorn").
  - **"Pause for a Cause"** is a clever anti-cheat feature, since if you need to bypass a block, you must donate to the World Wildlife Fund for a 10-minute break. Cold Turkey publishes these donation records publicly for transparency.
  - **"Good Bye Twitter!"** feature shows a motivational quote when you visit a blocked site (for example: "You can't use up creativity. The more you use, the more you have. - Maya Angelou"), which is a more positive approach than just showing an error.
  - It's a **one-time purchase** (no subscription) and stores all data locally and nothing is sent to their servers.
  - **Limitation:** Desktop only. No mobile app. This is a big gap compared to Freedom and Focus Bear.

### Tiimo (https://www.tiimoapp.com)
- **What I found:** Tiimo describes itself as "A visual AI planner built for real life." In fact, it won the **Apple iPhone App of the Year 2025**, which shows significant recognition. It's specifically designed for neurodivergent users but is usable by anyone.
- **Key observations:**
  - **AI-powered planning** is Tiimo's standout feature, you can type or speak what's on your mind, and the AI breaks tasks down into steps, estimates time for each, and organizes them into a visual schedule.
  - **Visual timeline** with color-coded blocks and icons makes your day instantly scannable. You can see what's coming up at a glance without reading text-heavy lists.
  - **Executive functioning toolkit** includes a live countdown timer that keeps you anchored to the current task, helping with task initiation and task switching, which are common struggles for people with ADHD.
  - **Mood tracking** lets you log how you feel, spot patterns, and adjust routines accordingly. This is a wellness feature that neither Freedom, Cold Turkey, nor Focus Bear offers.
  - **Customization**: You can choose colors, icons, and layouts to reflect how you work best. Personalizing your planner builds motivation and makes it easier to keep coming back.
  - **Widgets** show what's next without opening the app, reducing friction.
  - **Cross-platform sync** between desktop web app, phone, tablet, and Apple Watch.
  - **No distraction blocking** since Tiimo focuses entirely on planning and structure, not on actively blocking websites or apps. This is its biggest gap compared to Focus Bear.

### Routinery (https://routinery.co)
- **What I found:** Routinery is a daily routine planner recognized by Apple as an "App to Support Those with ADHD." It focuses on step-by-step guided routines with timers.
- **Key observations:**
  - **800+ to-do icons** make routine building visual and engaging, you can assign specific icons to each task, which adds visual variety that text-only planners lack.
  - **Guided timers with voice alerts and push notifications** walk you through each step of a routine, telling you when to move to the next task. If a task overruns, the timer keeps counting so you can see exactly how much you've gone over, which directly addresses *time blindness*, a common ADHD challenge.
  - **Flexibility during routines**: You can pause, skip, adjust times, or add new tasks on the fly without disrupting the entire routine. This is important because rigid routines often fail for ADHD users.
  - **Pomodoro tool** is built in for focused work sessions.
  - **Progress tracking** with daily/weekly/monthly views and "plant badges" for streaks adds a gamification element to maintain motivation.
  - **Focus mode** can block other apps during a routine, though this is a secondary feature rather than its core purpose.
  - Starts at **$3.99/month** for premium.

## Competitive Analysis Questions

**What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?**

Focus Bear is unique since it *combines three capabilities* that no single competitor offers together:
1. **Active distraction blocking across all devices** (like Freedom and Cold Turkey, but with cross-device support that Cold Turkey lacks)
2. **Real-time habit guidance** that walks you through your habits step-by-step while blocking distractions (unlike Tiimo and Routinery which focus on planning but don't actively block distractions during routines)
3. **Built by a neurodivergent team** (founder and many team members have ADHD + ASD) giving the app a "lived experience" design philosophy that general productivity apps like Freedom don't have

For example, Focus Bear's morning routine feature doesn't just remind you to exercise, it *plays a follow-along YouTube HIIT video* while blocking all other distracting YouTube content, and your entire computer and phone stay locked until you finish your routine. While Freedom can block YouTube entirely, it can't selectively allow guided workout videos. Tiimo can schedule your morning routine visually, but won't block Instagram from derailing you. As for Cold Turkey, it can lock your computer completely, but can't guide you through habits in real time.

Additionally, Focus Bear's *AI Blocking* feature is unique since instead of requiring you to manually create blocklists, you simply write your focus intentions and the AI determines which sites are relevant and blocks everything else. Freedom requires manually curated blocklists, and Cold Turkey requires specifying exact URLs or wildcards.

**If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?**

I would choose Focus Bear over competitors because it solves the *whole problem* rather than just one piece of it. My main productivity challenges involve both getting distracted by apps/websites and failing to follow through on good habits. Here's how the competitors fall short for someone like me:
- **Freedom** can block distractions effectively across devices, but once the blocking session ends, there's nothing guiding me toward productive habits. I could unblock and immediately go back to scrolling.
- **Cold Turkey** is extremely strict (even offering Frozen Turkey to lock my entire computer), but it's desktop-only and doesn't help me build positive routines outside my desktop.
- **Tiimo** is beautifully designed with its visual timeline and AI planning, but it doesn't actually block distractions. If I see an Instagram notification while following my Tiimo schedule, there's nothing stopping me from opening it.
- **Routinery** guides me through routines step-by-step, but its focus blocking is limited and it doesn't offer cross-device blocking.

Focus Bear fills all these gaps as it blocks distractions while guiding me through habits. It works also across my computer and phone simultaneously. Especially the "Late No More" feature which escalates meeting notifications from visual popups to verbal alerts as the start time approaches is something I haven't found in any competitor. It's specifically designed for ADHD brains that tend to ignore standard calendar notifications.

**What's one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn't?**

Cold Turkey's "Frozen Turkey" feature is a full computer lock/log-off/shutdown mode. This goes beyond just blocking apps and websites because it physically forces you away from your workstation on a schedule. For someone who hyperfocuses for unhealthy amounts of time, this could be incredibly useful as a hard-stop mechanism. Focus Bear offers distraction blocking and break reminders (via its Smart Break feature), but it doesn't have the stricter option of locking you out of your entire computer or forcing a shutdown.

Another feature Focus Bear doesn't have is Tiimo's mood tracking. Tiimo lets users log how they feel throughout the day, spot patterns over time, and correlate moods with specific routines or tasks. This kind of emotional data could help Focus Bear users understand why certain routines work better on some days and adjust their habits accordingly.

**Based on your research, what's one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?**

Based on my research, Focus Bear can improve its user friendliness and UI/UX design to cater to a wider range of users, especially the ones who aren't inclined in to techs in general. An example of this is Tiimo since it introduces an easy-to-use interface for users to create and manage their routines.

Looking at Tiimo specifically, here are examples of what Focus Bear could adopt:
- **Visual timeline with color-coded blocks** — Tiimo displays your entire day as a visual timeline with color-coded blocks and icons, so users can see their schedule at a glance without navigating through text-heavy menus. Focus Bear could adopt a similar layout for its routines and focus sessions, making the daily plan instantly scannable.
- **AI-assisted setup** — Tiimo lets users type or speak what they need to do, and the AI automatically breaks it into steps and builds a schedule. Focus Bear could use a similar guided onboarding so new users don't have to manually configure every habit and blocklist from scratch.
- **Personalization with colors and icons** — Tiimo lets users choose custom colors, icons, and layouts for their planner, which builds a sense of ownership and makes the app feel less intimidating. Adding similar customization to Focus Bear's routines would make the experience more inviting for non-technical users.
- **At-a-glance widgets** — Tiimo's widgets show what's coming up next without opening the app, reducing friction. A Focus Bear widget showing the current routine step or next focus session would help users stay on track effortlessly.

Tiimo's success gave them the award of winning the Apple iPhone App of the Year 2025 with over 500,000 users. This shows that an intuitive, visually driven interface resonates strongly with neurodivergent users. By adopting similar UI/UX principles, Focus Bear could lower its barrier to entry while keeping its powerful distraction-blocking and habit-building features accessible to everyone.
